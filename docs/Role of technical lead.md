# Tech Lead Role Description

The role of a Technical Lead (or Tech Lead) is a unique hybrid that sits at the intersection of software engineering, architecture, and team leadership.
A Tech Lead is primarily responsible for the technical vision and the quality of the code produced by the team.
Think of them as the "playing coach"—they are still in the trenches writing code, but they are also responsible for the overall success of the project.

## Core Responsibilities

### 1. Technical Vision and Architecture

The Tech Lead ensures the team isn't just writing code, but building a sustainable system. They make the "big" decisions that are hard to undo later.

- System Design: Defining how different services or modules interact.
- Tech Stack Selection: Evaluating which frameworks, databases, or languages best suit the problem.
- Standards: Setting the bar for code quality, testing patterns, and documentation.

### 2. Mentorship and Team Growth

The growth of their team members reflects a Tech Lead’s success.

- Code Reviews: Using PRs as a teaching tool rather than just a gatekeeping mechanism.
- Pair Programming: Working through complex bugs with junior or mid-level devs.
- Knowledge Sharing: Hosting "brown bag" sessions or technical demos to keep the team's skills sharp.

### 3. Bridging the Gap (The Translator)

They act as a buffer and a translator between technical and non-technical stakeholders.

- Product Alignment: Working with Product Managers to explain why a feature might take three weeks instead of three days.
- Risk Management: Identifying technical debt or security risks before they become "fires."

## Technical Lead vs. Engineering Manager

While the titles are sometimes blurred, the focus is generally distinct:

| Feature | Technical lead | Technical lead |  
| -- | -- | -- |
| Primary Focus | Technical "How" & Quality | People "Who" & Strategy |
| Daily Activity | Coding, Architecture, PRs | 1:1s, Hiring, Budgeting |
| Success Metric | System Reliability & Performance | Team Happiness & Delivery |

## Key Skills for Success

- Deep Technical Expertise: You don’t have to be the best coder, but you need the respect of the team and the ability to debug the hardest problems.
- Pragmatism: The ability to choose "good enough" over "perfect" to meet a business deadline.
- Empathy: Understanding that behind every line of code is a human who might be tired, frustrated, or confused.

The Tech Lead Paradox: The more successful you are at leveling up your team, the less "critical" your own daily coding becomes. Your goal is to build a team that can eventually function brilliantly without you.
